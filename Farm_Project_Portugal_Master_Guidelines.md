# Farm Project Portugal - Master Guidelines

## Mission

Design, evaluate, and develop a legally compliant, economically viable,
and highly self-sufficient farm capable of supporting a large family
(target: \~10 people) while generating diversified income.

## Core Principles

-   Prioritize legal compliance over loopholes.
-   Optimize for long-term resilience.
-   Diversify production.
-   Phase investments from fast ROI to long-term systems.
-   Base every decision on data.

# Research Roadmap

## Phase 1 -- Legal Framework

Research: - National planning rules. - Municipal PDM. - Farmer's
dwelling requirements. - Existing building renovation. - Protected areas
(REN, RAN, Natura 2000). - Water rights. - Agricultural licensing. -
Food processing regulations. - Rural tourism.

Deliverable: A decision tree explaining what can legally be built and
under which conditions.

## Phase 3 -- Environmental Analysis

Evaluate:

### Climate

-   USDA hardiness equivalent
-   Average temperatures
-   Frost dates
-   Heat waves
-   Rainfall
-   Drought frequency
-   Wind exposure
-   Solar exposure
-   Growing degree days

### Soil

-   Texture
-   Depth
-   Drainage
-   Organic matter
-   pH
-   Fertility
-   Compaction
-   Rock content
-   Erosion risk
-   Water holding capacity

### Water

-   Wells
-   Boreholes
-   Streams
-   Reservoirs
-   Irrigation
-   Seasonal availability

Output: Map every enterprise to environmental suitability:

Example:

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Environment                         Best Activities
  ----------------------------------- -----------------------------------
  Poor rocky soil                     Goats, sheep, beekeeping, aromatic
                                      herbs, agroforestry

  Deep fertile soil                   Vegetables, orchards, mushrooms,
                                      poultry

  Dry hot climate                     Olives, almonds, figs, sheep

  Cool humid                          Mushrooms, chestnuts, berries
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 4 -- Ideal Farm Design

Design: - Residential area - Farm Operations Centre - Workshops -
Processing - Storage - Animal zones - Orchards - Agroforestry - Water
systems - Renewable energy - Future tourism

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 5 -- Land Evaluation System

Score every property (100 points):

-   Legal viability (20)
-   Water resources (20)
-   Soil quality (15)
-   Climate suitability (10)
-   Existing buildings (10)
-   Infrastructure (10)
-   Enterprise compatibility (10)
-   Expansion potential (5)

Every evaluation should include: - Risks - Opportunities - Estimated
investment - 10-year roadmap

# Claude Skills

## Skill: Legal Analyst

Determine: - PDM classification - Buildability - Restrictions - Required
permits - Residential feasibility

## Skill: Environmental Analyst

Analyse: - Climate - Soil - Water - Topography - Suitability for each
enterprise

Return a ranked compatibility matrix.

## Skill: Enterprise Planner

Recommend enterprises based on: - Legal constraints - Climate - Soil -
Water - Budget - Labour - ROI - Synergies

## Skill: Land Evaluator

Produce: - Overall score - SWOT - Development phases - Estimated costs -
Priority actions

## Skill: Farm Systems Architect

Design integrated systems: - Agroforestry - Rotational grazing - Water
harvesting - Renewable energy - Waste cycling - Food production -
Processing

## Skill: Financial Planner

Estimate: - CAPEX - OPEX - Cash flow - Payback - Profitability - Grant
opportunities

## Skill: Research Assistant

Always: - Prefer official Portuguese sources. - Distinguish national law
from municipality rules. - Flag uncertainty. - Cite sources. - Suggest
follow-up research.

# Working Rules

Whenever a property is analysed: 1. Verify legal status. 2. Analyse
environment (soil, climate, water). 3. Identify suitable enterprises. 4.
Score the property. 5. Produce a phased implementation plan. 6.
Highlight unknowns before making assumptions.

The objective is to continuously build a structured knowledge base that
improves every future decision.
