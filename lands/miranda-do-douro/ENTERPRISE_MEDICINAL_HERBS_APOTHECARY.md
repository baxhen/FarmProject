## Why Medicinal Herbs?

Culinary herbs (rosemary, thyme, oregano) are already in every Miranda enterprise plan. Medicinal herbs add:
1. **Higher value per m²**: Dried medicinal herbs €20-60/kg vs culinary €15-35/kg
2. **Product diversification**: Teas, tinctures, salves, hydrosols, bath products
3. **Workshop/tourism draw**: "Make Your Own Medicine" is a premium experience
4. **Multiple revenue streams from same plants**: Fresh → dried → tincture → salve → teaching
5. **Year-round sales**: Wellness products sell steadily (not seasonal like fresh produce)

## Climate Matching — Miranda vs Perais

| Herb | Miranda (Csb) | Perais (Csa) | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Calendula | Excellent | Excellent | Annual, grows anywhere |
| Chamomile | Excellent | Good | Prefers cooler summers |
| Echinacea | Excellent | Good | Cold-hardy, needs winter chill |
| Lemon Balm | Excellent | Good | Hardy, may struggle in extreme heat |
| Peppermint | Excellent | Good | Needs moisture — streamside |
| Yarrow | Excellent | Excellent | Drought-tolerant, any soil |
| Lavender | Excellent | Excellent | Loves hot dry summers |
| Rosemary | Good | Excellent | May freeze in harsh Miranda winter |
| Holy Basil (Tulsi) | Poor (frost) | **Excellent** | Annual in warm climate only |
| Ashwagandha | Poor (frost) | **Excellent** | Needs 4-5 month warm season |
| Valerian | Excellent | Poor (needs chill) | Cold-stratified perennial |
| Elderberry | Excellent | Good | Hardy shrub, needs water |
| St. John's Wort | Excellent | Good | Cold-hardy, drought-tolerant |
| Comfrey | Excellent | Good | "Bocking 14" sterile cultivar |

**Strategy**: Grow cold-hardy medicinals (valerian, echinacea, elderberry, St. John's wort) in Miranda. Grow warm-climate adaptogens (holy basil, ashwagandha) in Perais. Two-terroir medicinal product line.

## Regulatory Pathway

| Product | Regulation | Requirements |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Dried herbs (bundles, loose) | Tier 1 | Agricultural registration only. No health claims on label. |
| Herbal tea blends | Tier 1 | Same as dried herbs. "Herbal infusion" not "medicine." |
| Tinctures (alcohol-based) | Grey area | As food supplement: notify DGAV. No medical claims. |
| Salves/balms (oil-based, no water) | Tier 4 (cosmetics) | CPSR required! Anhydrous products may have simpler safety assessment. |
| Hydrosols (water-based) | Tier 4 (cosmetics) | CPSR required. Preservative system needed. |
| Bath products (salts, soaks) | Tier 4 (cosmetics) | CPSR required. |

**Start Tier 1 only** (dried herbs, teas). Add Tier 4 products after registered kitchen + CPSR. **Never make medical claims** — market as "wellness," "traditional," "aromatherapy."
