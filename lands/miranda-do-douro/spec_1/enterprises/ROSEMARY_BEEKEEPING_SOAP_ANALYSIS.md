### Soap Production Regulations (Portugal)

**Legal Requirements**:

1. **Cosmetics Regulation (EU 1223/2009)**
   - ALL soap for sale = cosmetic product
   - Must comply with safety assessment
   - Required: Safety report by qualified person (chemist, pharmacist)
   - Cost: €300-800 (one-time, covers multiple formulas)

2. **CPNP Registration** (Cosmetic Products Notification Portal)
   - Register each soap formula with EU portal
   - Free, but requires safety assessment first
   - Allows legal sale throughout EU

3. **Labeling Requirements**:
   - INCI ingredient list (standardized names)
   - Net weight
   - Best before date (typically 24 months)
   - Contact information
   - Warnings (if applicable)

4. **GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice)**:
   - Clean production area (dedicated room ideal)
   - Record-keeping (batch numbers, traceability)
   - NOT required to be certified GMP (for small producers), but must follow principles

**Bio/Organic Certification** (Optional but Valuable):

**Ecocert** (most recognized in Portugal/Europe):
- Requirements:
  - 95%+ organic ingredients
  - No synthetic fragrances, colorants, or preservatives
  - Environmentally-friendly production
- Cost: €500-1,500/year (inspection + certification)
- Benefit: 30-50% price premium + access to organic stores

**Processo**:
1. Develop 3-5 soap formulas
2. Hire qualified person for safety assessment (€300-800)
3. Register formulas in CPNP
4. Begin production (follow GMP)
5. Optional: Apply for organic certification (Year 2-3, once established)

**Timeline**: 2-4 months (safety assessment → registration → production)

## PART 9: RISKS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Soap Regulations Too Complex

**Likelihood**: 20% (regulations exist, but manageable for small producers)

**Mitigation**:
- Hire qualified person for safety assessment (€300-800, one-time)
- Join Portuguese soap makers association (support, knowledge sharing)
- Start small (500 bars Year 1), scale once compliant

**Fallback**: If regulations too burdensome, focus on rosemary + honey sales only (still profitable)

---

### Risk 2: Market Saturation (Too Many Soap Makers)

**Likelihood**: 30-40% (handmade soap market competitive)

**Mitigation**:
- Strong differentiation (YOUR rosemary, YOUR honey, YOUR farm story)
- Premium positioning (bio/organic, traceable)
- Geographic advantage (Miranda do Douro = unique, not Lisbon/Porto saturated market)
- Focus on experience (workshops, farm visits) not just product

**Fallback**: Reduce soap production, increase rosemary/honey sales

---

### Risk 3: Rosemary Crop Failure

**Likelihood**: 10% (rosemary very hardy, drought-tolerant)

**Potential Causes**: Overwatering (root rot), extreme cold (<-15°C), disease

**Mitigation**:
- Excellent drainage (plant on slope, rocky soil)
- Minimal irrigation (only Year 1, then rain-fed)
- Monitor for disease (rare in rosemary)
- Diversify plantings (670 plants = if 10% die, still have 600)

**Impact**: If 30% crop loss, still produce enough for soap + sales

---

### Risk 4: Time Overwhelm

**Likelihood**: 40% (rosemary + bees + soap + other enterprises = demanding)

**Mitigation**:
- Phase implementation (Year 1 rosemary, Year 2 soap, Year 3 scale)
- Batch soap production (1 day/month, make 200 bars)
- Hire part-time help (Year 3+, €600-1,200/year)
- Automate where possible (drip irrigation, curing racks)

**Warning Signs**: If consistently working >50 hours/week, reduce soap production OR hire help
