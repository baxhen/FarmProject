## 4. The Stone Walls — Hidden Value

Traditional Portuguese stone walls ("paredes de pedra") are:
- Permanent: last 100-200+ years with zero maintenance
- Ecological: habitat for lizards, insects, small mammals
- Thermal: heat storage moderates microclimate near walls
- Cultural: part of Trás-os-Montes agricultural heritage
- Expensive to build today: €50-100/linear meter for skilled dry-stone work
- Property is "totalmente demarcado por parede em toda a sua extenção" — fully enclosed

**Estimated wall length** (1.43ha rectangular): ~480-520 linear meters
**Replacement value**: €24,000-52,000 at current rates
**This single feature may exceed the property purchase price.**

## 7. Water Strategy

**Critical unknown**: No water source mentioned.

**Options** (in order of likelihood):

1. **Public water from Sendim**: Property is near village. Connection cost €1,000-3,000. Monthly bill €20-40. Most reliable option.

2. **Well drilling**: Standard for Miranda region. Depth typically 80-150m. Cost €3,000-8,000. Need APA approval. Success rate in Planalto Mirandês: moderate (schist aquifer).

3. **Rainwater harvesting**: 400-500mm rain × building roof area. Supplementary only — cannot meet full irrigation needs.

4. **Water delivery**: Tank delivery for critical periods. Expensive per liter, viable for small vegetable garden only.

**Recommendation**: Confirm public water availability from Sendim before purchase. If public water available, score rises to 73-75. If only well possible, factor €5,000-8,000 into purchase budget.
