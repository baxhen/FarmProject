## Enterprise 1: Eggs — Orchard-Rotation Chickens

**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5) — Ideal starter enterprise for spec_2

**Scale**: 15 laying hens in mobile coop, rotated through orchard
**Space**: 200m² active + 1,500m² orchard rotation
**Investment**: €250-400 (coop + pullets + initial feed)
**Time to revenue**: 2-4 weeks (point-of-lay pullets)

**Production**:
- 10-13 eggs/day = 3,500-4,500 eggs/year
- Family consumption: 1,500-2,000 eggs/year
- Surplus for sale: 1,500-2,500 eggs/year = €375-875/year

**Synergies**:
- Pest control under fruit trees (insects, fallen fruit)
- Fertilization (manure directly on orchard floor)
- Weed management (scratching)
- Compost accelerator (manure + bedding = compost feedstock)
- Orchard provides shade + forage

**Labor**: 15 min/day (feed, water, collect eggs, move coop). 2 hrs/week deep clean.

**Self-Sufficiency Value**: €600-800/year (eggs not purchased at €0.40/egg)

## Enterprise 2: Intensive Vegetables — Market Garden

**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐½ (4.5/5) — Flat land + public water = ideal

**Scale**: 300m² seasonal production + 350m² kitchen garden
**Investment**: €300-600 (seeds, amendments, drip tape, row cover)
**Time to revenue**: 30-60 days (fast crops), 60-90 days (main crops)

**Production Model** — Biointensive, no-till, permanent beds:
- 15 beds × 1m × 10m = 150m² growing area + paths
- Crop rotation: Leaf → Fruit → Root → Legume
- Succession planting: 2-4 crops/bed/year
- Season extension: Row cover for frost, shade cloth for summer

**Crop Selection by Season**:

**Spring (Mar-May)**:
- Radish, salad mix, arugula, spinach, peas, fava beans
- Revenue: €300-600

**Summer (Jun-Aug)**:
- Tomatoes, peppers, zucchini, beans, chard, cucumbers
- Revenue: €500-1,000

**Fall (Sep-Nov)**:
- Brassicas (kale, cabbage, broccoli), carrots, beets, leeks
- Revenue: €300-600

**Winter (Dec-Feb)**:
- Protected: mâche, claytonia, winter lettuce, spinach
- Unprotected: garlic (planted Oct), fava beans
- Revenue: €100-300

**Total Year 1 Revenue**: €1,200-2,500
**Self-Sufficiency Value**: €600-1,200 (family vegetables year-round)

**Advantages of Spec 2 for Vegetables**:
- Flat = easy bed layout, no terracing, wheelbarrow-friendly
- Public water = reliable irrigation, no pump needed
- Fenced = deer/boar protection (critical in Trás-os-Montes)
- Near city = short supply chain, fresh delivery possible

## Enterprise 6: Vineyard — PDM Heritage Crop

**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐¼ (4.3/5)

**Scale**: 500m², ~250 vines
**Investment**: €500-1,000 (vines + trellis posts + wire)
**Time to revenue**: Year 3 (first light harvest), Year 5 (full production)

**Why Grapes on Spec 2**:
- PDM designation "Vinhas" — this land was classified for vineyards in the municipal plan
- Douro region = world heritage wine terroir
- 800-1,200 chill hours = excellent for quality wine grapes
- Hot dry summers = concentrated flavors, less fungal disease
- Flat terrain = easy trellising, mechanization possible

**Recommended Varieties** (Douro traditional):
- Touriga Nacional (red): Premium Port + table wine
- Tinta Roriz / Tempranillo (red): Adaptable, reliable
- Malvasia Fina (white): Traditional Douro white
- Table grapes for fresh market: lower risk entry point

**Production** (Year 5+, 250 vines):
- Wine grapes: 500-750kg → 350-500 bottles (home production) or €500-1,500 grape sales
- Table grapes: €1,000-3,000 at Miranda market

**Revenue (Year 3+)**: €1,000-5,000/year

**Note**: Wine production requires licensing. Table grapes avoid this. Start with table grapes, transition to wine if market + legal pathway clear.

## Enterprise 7: Beekeeping — Pollination + Premium Honey

**Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐¾ (4.8/5)

**Scale**: 2-4 hives at property edge (50m²)
**Investment**: €550-850 (hives + nucs + equipment)
**Time to revenue**: Year 1 minimal, Year 2+ full

**Forage Calendar** (what bees eat, when):
- Feb: Almond blossom (EARLY forage = rare, valuable)
- Mar-Apr: Fruit tree blossom (apple, cherry, plum, peach)
- May-Jun: Herbs (rosemary, thyme, lavender), wild vegetation
- Jul-Aug: Summer wildflowers, garden crops
- Sep-Oct: Late wild vegetation

**Monofloral Honey Potential**:
- Almond honey: Rare, early-season. Distinctive bitter-sweet. Premium €15-25/kg.
- Rosemary honey: Classic Mediterranean. Light, aromatic. €12-18/kg.
- Wildflower: Complex, terroir-driven. €8-12/kg.

**Production** (Year 2+, 2-4 hives):
- 40-100kg honey/year
- Revenue: €400-2,000/year (depending on monofloral vs wildflower)
- Wax, propolis: €50-200/year

**Pollination Benefit** (often overlooked):
- Fruit trees: +20-30% yield from bee pollination
- Herbs: +20-30% essential oil content
- Vegetables: +10-20% yield (cucurbits especially)
- **This is the hidden value — bees make every other enterprise more productive**

**Self-Sufficiency**: 5-10kg honey/year for family. Value: €75-150.
