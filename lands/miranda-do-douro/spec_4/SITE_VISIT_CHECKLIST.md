## 3. Water Verification

### Stream (MOST VALUABLE ASSET)
- [ ] **Flow rate**: Measure. Use bucket + timer at narrow point. L/min in summer?
- [ ] **Seasonal variation**: Ask neighbors — does stream flow year-round? August flow?
- [ ] **Water quality**: Collect sample. Livestock-safe? Irrigation-safe? Potable after treatment?
- [ ] **Elevation drop**: Walk stream length on property. Height difference start to end? (gravity irrigation potential)
- [ ] **Ram pump viability**: If elevation drop >1m, ram pump can lift water without electricity
- [ ] **Riparian zone**: Width? Vegetation? Flood evidence?
- [ ] **Water rights**: Legal to use for irrigation? Livestock? Domestic (after treatment)?

### Lameiro (Wet Meadow)
- [ ] **Area**: How many m² is the lameiro? (estimate from vegetation — greener, lusher grass)
- [ ] **Water table**: How close to surface? Dig test hole. Year-round moisture?
- [ ] **Hay potential**: How many cuts per year? (lameiro typically 1-2 cuts)
- [ ] **Grazing capacity**: How many sheep could this support? (wet meadow = high carrying capacity)

---

## 4. Soil Testing

- [ ] **Lameiro soil**: pH, organic matter. Typically fertile — verify.
- [ ] **Cultivation area ("área para cultivo")**: Where is it? pH, texture, depth.
- [ ] **Forest soil**: Under trees. Likely acidic (oak leaf litter). Suitable for what?
- [ ] **EN218 edge**: Soil near road — any contamination concerns? (unlikely on rural road)

## 5. Existing Resources

### Stream Corridor
- [ ] **Width**: Walk both banks. Map the stream path.
- [ ] **Vegetation**: Riparian species? Willows? Alders? Invasives?
- [ ] **Wildlife signs**: Tracks, nests, birds. (Tourism asset — birdwatching)
- [ ] **Shade**: Tree cover over stream? (keeps water cool in summer)

### Lameiro
- [ ] **Grass species**: What's growing? (indicates soil + moisture)
- [ ] **Drainage**: Any standing water? Too wet or just right?
- [ ] **Current use**: Grazed? Hay cut? Abandoned?

### Trees
- [ ] **Count + species**: Any cork oaks? Holm oaks? Other?
- [ ] **Firewood potential**: Sustainable harvest without clearing?
- [ ] **Forest management plan required?**: ICNF may require one for Espaço Florestal

### EN218 Frontage
- [ ] **Exact frontage meters**: Measure. 100m per listing — verify.
- [ ] **Visibility from road**: Can cars see a farm stand? Speed limit? (visibility = sales)
- [ ] **Access point**: Existing entrance? Need to create one? (câmara approval for new EN218 access)
- [ ] **Noise**: Traffic noise level at building site? (mitigation: trees, distance)

## 8. Red Flags

| Red Flag | Severity | Check |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Stream dries in summer | **CRITICAL** | If stream is seasonal, property loses its main asset. Visit in August. |
| Building impossible in forest zone | **CRITICAL** | PIP may reveal "possibilidades" means "no." Verify before offer. |
| Lameiro is protected wetland | **HIGH** | If classified as zona húmida, drainage/modification prohibited. |
| Forest fire mandatory clearing costs | MEDIUM | Espaço Florestal = legal requirement to manage fire risk. Annual cost? |
| EN218 access permit denied | MEDIUM | New access to national road needs Estradas de Portugal approval. |

## 9. Visit Logistics

**Best time**: **August** — stream at lowest flow (survival test), fire risk visible, lameiro at driest. OR **February** — stream at highest, lameiro waterlogged, assess winter conditions.

**Bring**: Flow measurement tools (bucket + timer), soil kit, GPS, camera, waders/waterproof boots (stream + lameiro), EN218 traffic counter (optional — count cars for farm shop viability).

**Duration**: 4-5 hours. 3ha + stream walk + lameiro assessment + EN218 evaluation.

**After visit**: PIP to câmara (CRITICAL). Water quality lab test. Electricity connection quote. ICNF forest management requirements.
