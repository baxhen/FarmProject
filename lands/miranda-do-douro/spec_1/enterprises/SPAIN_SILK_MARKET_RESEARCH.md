## SPAIN SILK HISTORY AND CURRENT STATE

### Historical Context

**Spain had a MAJOR silk industry** (16th-19th centuries):
- Granada, Murcia, Valencia were silk capitals of Europe
- Mulberry cultivation widespread
- Royal silk factories (Real Fábrica de Sedas)
- Moorish heritage of sericulture

**Collapse**: Late 19th-early 20th century (same as Portugal)
- Cheaper Asian silk
- Pebrine disease
- Industrial textile shift

**Current State**:
- ✓ Small artisan revival
- ✓ Cultural heritage projects
- ✓ Museum/educational programs
- ⚠ NO large-scale commercial industry (like Portugal)
- ⚠ BUT more infrastructure than Portugal

## REALISTIC SPANISH MARKET OPPORTUNITIES

### Option 1: **Artisan Silk Product Sales** (Not Cocoon Sales) ⭐⭐⭐

**Model**: You produce silk products (scarves, textiles, crafts), sell to Spanish artisan markets

**Potential Markets**:
- Valencia artisan shops/markets
- Granada tourist shops (Albaicín)
- Salamanca (closer, university town, artisan market)
- Madrid artisan fairs (3-4 hours from Miranda)

**Requirements**:
- ✓ You must reel and weave silk yourself (equipment €10,000-20,000)
- ✓ You must develop artisan skills (2-3 years learning)
- ✓ You must create high-quality products (€50-300 per item)
- ✓ You must travel to markets or sell online

**Revenue Potential**: €3,000-10,000/year IF successful artisan

**Is this better than selling in Portugal?**: Slightly - Spain has more silk heritage awareness, but NOT dramatically better

**Verdict**: **Marginal improvement over Portugal-only market**. Spain adds maybe 20-30% more market opportunity, but still requires you to be artisan producer.
