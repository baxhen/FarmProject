# UP-1: Enterprise Evaluation Framework

**Principle**: Evaluate every enterprise across 11 standardized criteria (0-5 scale). Average the scores. Prioritize by overall score × synergy multiplier.

## The 11 Universal Criteria

1. Climate Suitability — Does the local climate support this?
2. Soil Suitability — Can soil (or amended soil) support this?
3. Water Suitability — Is water available in sufficient quantity?
4. Legal Simplicity — How complex are regulations and licensing?
5. Infrastructure Requirement — What buildings/equipment needed?
6. Labor Demand — Daily/seasonal work required?
7. Fast-Return Potential — When does revenue begin?
8. Long-Term Value — Does this appreciate over time?
9. Self-Sufficiency Value — Does this meet household needs?
10. Market Potential — Is there demand at viable prices?
11. Permaculture Synergy — Does this integrate with other enterprises?

## Scoring Guide

- 5 = Ideal/Excellent
- 4 = Very Good
- 3 = Adequate/Moderate
- 2 = Marginal/Challenging
- 1 = Poor/High Risk
- 0 = Incompatible/Impossible

## Interpretation

| Score | Recommendation |
|-------|---------------|
| 4.0-5.0 | Highly Recommended |
| 3.0-3.9 | Suitable |
| 2.0-2.9 | Marginal |
| 0-1.9 | Not Recommended |

## Timeline-Based Diversification

- Fast-Return (0-12 months): Eggs, vegetables, herbs, rabbits, microgreens, seedlings
- Medium-Term (1-3 years): Berries, bees, mushrooms, goats, fruit bushes, hardy kiwi
- Long-Term (3-10+ years): Nuts, olives, timber, cork, vineyards, agroforestry

## Synergy Multiplier (v0.3)

| Level | Multiplier | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| None | 1.0× | Standalone |
| Weak | 1.1× | Share infrastructure |
| Moderate | 1.2-1.3× | Share inputs/outputs |
| Strong | 1.4-1.5× | One feeds another |
| Exceptional | 1.6-2.0× | Multiple outputs from same input |

## Niche Market Types

1. Demographic — specific community (Brazilian immigrants 200,000+ in Portugal)
2. Product Gap — exists globally, not locally (hardy kiwi, honeyberry)
3. Quality — premium version of commodity (monofloral honey)
4. Experience — people pay to learn (workshops, foraging walks)
5. Geographic — location advantage (border proximity, EN218 frontage)

## Test-Then-Scale

- Phase 1 — Test (50-200m², 1-2 seasons, €50-500)
- Phase 2 — Pilot (200-500m², 1-2 seasons, €200-1,500)
- Phase 3 — Commercial (full scale)
- Kill criteria: Yield <50% expected, zero market interest, labor >2× expected
