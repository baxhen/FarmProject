## Tourism Model: Workshops + Farm Experiences (No Accommodation)

### Legal Framework — Portugal

**Workshop-only activities** (no overnight stays):
- **No tourism license required** (educational/agricultural activity)
- Civil liability insurance recommended (€150-300/year)
- Receipt issuing (recibos verdes or business entity)
- If serving food: additional HACCP requirements (simplified for occasional events)
- If selling products during workshop: normal direct sale rules apply

**This is the lowest-barrier tourism entry point.**

### Workshop Offerings

**1. Herb Harvest & Drying Workshop** (3 hours, €25-35/person)
- Walk through herb garden, identify medicinal/culinary herbs
- Harvest techniques for rosemary, thyme, oregano, lavender
- Drying methods: solar, dehydrator, hanging
- Each participant makes: herb bundle + tea blend to take home
- Max group: 8-10 people
- Season: May-September
- Revenue per workshop: €200-350

**2. Introduction to Beekeeping** (3 hours, €35-50/person)
- Bee biology and hive dynamics
- Suit up, open hive, identify queen/workers/drones
- Honey tasting: almond honey vs rosemary honey vs wildflower
- Each participant takes: 125g jar of farm honey
- Max group: 6-8 people (limited by bee suits)
- Season: April-September
- Revenue per workshop: €210-400

**3. Farm-to-Table Cooking** (4 hours, €45-75/person)
- Harvest vegetables + herbs from garden
- Cook traditional Portuguese dishes with farm ingredients
- Meal together under almond trees
- Each participant takes: recipe cards + herb bundle
- Max group: 8-10 people
- Season: May-October
- Revenue per workshop: €360-750
- Note: Requires HACCP for food service. Use existing building kitchen.

**4. Soap Making with Farm Botanicals** (3 hours, €35-55/person)
- Cold process soap making basics
- Use farm rosemary, lavender, honey, beeswax
- Each participant makes: 4-6 bars to take home + cure
- Max group: 6-8 people
- Season: Year-round (indoor activity)
- Revenue per workshop: €210-440
- Note: If soap sold to participants, cosmetic regulations apply to take-home product.

**5. Orchard & Vineyard Tour + Tasting** (2 hours, €15-25/person)
- Walk through almond orchard and vineyard
- Seasonal: almond blossom (Feb), fruit harvest (Jun-Sep), grape harvest (Sep-Oct)
- Tasting: almonds, dried fruit, farm honey, grape juice (or wine if licensed)
- Max group: 15-20 people
- Season: Year-round (different experience each season)
- Revenue per workshop: €225-500

## Marketing Strategy

**Target Audiences**:
1. **Miranda do Douro residents** — locals interested in traditional skills, gardening
2. **Douro tourists** — visitors to Douro wine region looking for authentic experiences
3. **Spanish day-trippers** — Zamora/Salamanca residents (30-60 min drive)
4. **Brazilian community in Portugal** — Brazilian herb workshops as cultural connection
5. **Portuguese urbanites** — Porto residents seeking countryside weekend experiences

**Channels**:
- Airbnb Experiences (global reach, handles payments)
- Facebook/Instagram (visual — orchard + herbs + river)
- Miranda tourist office partnership
- Douro wine route cross-promotion
- Brazilian community WhatsApp/Facebook groups

**Pricing Strategy**:
- Slightly below Porto/Lisbon workshop prices (rural location = value positioning)
- "Local discount" for Miranda residents
- Group discount for 6+ people
- Gift certificates for holiday season

## Integration with Other Enterprises

Tourism is a **revenue multiplier**, not standalone:

| Enterprise | Tourism Link |
|-----------|-------------|
| Herbs | Workshop subject + participants buy dried herbs |
| Beekeeping | Workshop subject + participants buy honey |
| Orchard | Setting for all outdoor activities + fruit sales |
| Vegetables | Farm-to-table ingredients + take-home produce |
| Value-Added Products | Participants buy infused oils, teas, soaps after experiencing the farm |
| Vineyard | Future wine tasting experience (if licensed) |

**Customer journey**: Workshop attendee → buys farm products → follows on social media → becomes regular online customer → tells friends → books private event.

**Conversion estimate**: 30-50% of workshop attendees buy €15-40 in farm products on the day. 10-20% become repeat online customers.

**Product sales during workshops**: Add €500-2,000/year (Year 1-2) to €2,000-5,000/year (Year 3+).

| Factor | Spec 1 | Spec 2 |

| Accommodation | Possible (5ha, space) | Not viable (0.6ha, no space) |

| Workshop space | Must build | **Existing building + orchard** |

**Different models**: Spec 1 = accommodation-based (higher investment, higher ceiling). Spec 2 = day-visitor workshops (lower investment, faster start, similar ceiling from volume).

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Low bookings | Medium | Start with 1/month, scale based on demand. Airbnb Experiences = no upfront cost, pay per booking. |
| Weather cancellation | Medium | Indoor backup (Soap Making in building). Reschedule policy. |
| Food safety incident | Low | HACCP for Farm-to-Table. Insurance coverage. |
| Neighbor complaints | Low | Limit group sizes. No loud music. Finish by 6pm. |
| Regulatory change | Low | Monitor tourism law. Activity-only model is least regulated. |
