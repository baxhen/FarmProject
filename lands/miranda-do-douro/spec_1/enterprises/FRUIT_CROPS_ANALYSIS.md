# Fruit Crops Analysis - Miranda do Douro Property

**Climate**: Continental Mediterranean - Hot dry summers, cold winters, 400-500mm rain
**Strengths**: Excellent sun, gentle slope, 2 wells with water

### 1. **FIGS** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (HIGHEST RECOMMENDATION)

**Time to First Harvest**: 1-2 years (if planted as larger trees)
**Time to Good Production**: 2-3 years

**Why Perfect for Your Property**:
- ✓✓✓ FASTEST fruit tree (harvest Year 2!)
- ✓✓✓ DROUGHT TOLERANT (survives with minimal water)
- ✓✓✓ HEAT LOVING (thrives in 35°C+ summers)
- ✓✓✓ Cold hardy (-12°C, fine for Miranda)
- ✓✓ High value fresh AND dried
- ✓✓ Very low maintenance
- ✓ No major pest/disease issues

**Climate Score**: 5/5 - IDEAL for Miranda do Douro

**Market**:
- Fresh figs: €4-8/kg (premium, short season - high demand)
- Dried figs: €8-15/kg (stores well, year-round sales)
- Fig jam: €6-10/jar
- Strong local and tourist demand

**Production**:
- Mature tree: 20-50 kg/year
- 20 trees = 400-1,000 kg/year
- **Revenue**: €3,000-8,000/year (fresh + dried mix)

**Investment**: €300-600 (20 trees at €15-30 each)

**Labor**: Very low (pruning, harvest, drying)

**Harvest**: July-September (2 crops with some varieties)

**Advantages**:
- Fastest return of ANY tree crop
- Thrives in your climate without irrigation
- High value fresh and dried
- Easy to process (sun-dry or dehydrator)
- Long productive life (50+ years)
- Beautiful tree (shade, aesthetics)

**Recommended**: **PLANT 20-30 FIG TREES IN YEAR 1** - Zone 2-3

**Varieties for Miranda**:
- 'Lampeira' (Portuguese, black, excellent dried)
- 'Pingo de Mel' (Portuguese, very sweet)
- 'Brown Turkey' (reliable, two crops)
- Mix 2-3 varieties for extended season

## TIER 5: NOT RECOMMENDED ❌

---

### ❌ **PEACHES, NECTARINES, APRICOTS**

**Why NOT**:
- Late frost kills flowers (Miranda has April-May frost risk)
- High chill requirement conflicts with early bloom
- Very high pest/disease pressure (Mediterranean fruit fly, etc.)
- Labor-intensive (thinning, spraying, bagging)
- Short shelf-life

**Verdict**: Skip these

---

### ❌ **CHERRIES**

**Why NOT**:
- Late frost risk
- Birds devastate crop (netting entire trees impractical)
- Short harvest window
- High labor

**Verdict**: Skip

---

### ❌ **CITRUS** (Oranges, Lemons)

**Why NOT**:
- Too cold in winter for Miranda (-5°C kills citrus)
- Only viable in protected microclimate or greenhouse

**Verdict**: Skip (or 1-2 potted lemons for kitchen use only)
