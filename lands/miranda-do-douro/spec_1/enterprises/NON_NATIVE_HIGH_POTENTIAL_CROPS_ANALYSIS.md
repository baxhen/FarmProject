# Non-Native High-Potential Crops for Small-Scale Testing
## Miranda do Douro Property - Experimental Crop Analysis

**Date**: 2026-07-09
**Property**: 5 hectares, Miranda do Douro, Solo Rústico
**Climate**: Continental Mediterranean (hot dry summers, cold winters, 400-500mm rain)
**Strategy**: Test 50-200m² plots of non-traditional crops with high market potential

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Purpose**: Identify non-native crops that:
1. ✅ Can tolerate Miranda do Douro climate (hot summers, cold winters to -10°C)
2. ✅ Have HIGH market potential (niche markets, premium pricing, growing demand)
3. ✅ Can be tested small-scale (50-200m² = low risk, learning phase)
4. ✅ Could scale to profitable enterprise if successful (500-1,000m² future)

**Approach**: "Test-and-Scale" methodology
- Year 1: Plant 50-100m² experimental plot
- Evaluate: Survival, yield, quality, market interest
- Year 2-3: IF successful → Scale to 200-500m²
- Year 3+: IF very successful → Scale to 1,000m² OR integrate into main production

**Total Experimental Budget**: €500-1,500 (Year 1, for 5-10 test crops)
**Potential Revenue** (if 3-5 succeed): €5,000-15,000/year by Year 3

---

## SELECTION CRITERIA

Each crop rated 0-5 on:
1. **Climate Compatibility** (winter hardiness, summer heat tolerance)
2. **Market Potential** (demand, pricing, competition)
3. **Cultural Difficulty** (ease of growing, expertise needed)
4. **Scalability** (can it go from 50m² to 500m²+ profitably?)
5. **Uniqueness** (how rare in Portugal/Miranda do Douro region?)

**Minimum Score to Test**: 18/25 (3.6 average)
**Ideal Score**: 22+/25 (4.4+ average)

### 1. **GOJI BERRIES** (Lycium barbarum) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Origin**: China, Tibet
**Why Test**: Superfood trend, premium pricing, hardy plant

**Climate Compatibility**: **5/5** - EXCELLENT
- Hardy to -25°C (your winters easy)
- Tolerates heat, drought (perfect for continental climate)
- Thrives in poor soil
- 400-500mm rain = IDEAL (too much water = poor fruit quality)

**Market Potential**: **5/5** - EXCEPTIONAL
- Health food trend (antioxidants, "superfood" marketing)
- Fresh berries: €15-30/kg (when available - RARE fresh in Portugal)
- Dried berries: €40-80/kg retail (vs €20-30/kg imported from China)
- Growing demand (health food stores, Asian markets, smoothie shops)
- LOW competition (almost NO commercial production in Portugal)

**Cultural Difficulty**: **4/5** - EASY-MODERATE
- Perennial shrub (plant once, produce 10-20 years)
- Minimal care (drought-tolerant, pest-resistant)
- Prune annually for shape/production
- Begin fruiting Year 2-3, full production Year 4-5
- Challenge: Hand-harvest (labor-intensive, but high value compensates)

**Scalability**: **4/5** - GOOD
- 100 plants (100m²) = 30-80 kg berries/year (Year 4+) = €1,200-2,400 (dried)
- 500 plants (500m²) = 150-400 kg = €6,000-16,000/year
- Limiting factor: Hand-harvest labor (but seasonal, hire help during harvest)

**Uniqueness**: **5/5** - VERY RARE
- Almost no commercial goji production in Portugal
- Imported dried goji expensive (€30-50/kg retail)
- "Grown in Portugal" = premium positioning vs Chinese imports

**TOTAL SCORE**: **23/25** - HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

**Year 1 Test**:
- Plant: 20-30 plants (50m² plot)
- Investment: €100-200 (plants @ €4-8 each)
- Year 2-3: First berries (light harvest, 5-15 kg)
- Evaluate: Fruit quality, yield, market interest (test sales at farmers market)
- Decision Year 3: If successful, scale to 200-500 plants

**Expected Yield Timeline**:
- Year 1: Planting
- Year 2: First berries (0.5-1 kg/plant) = 10-30 kg total
- Year 3: Growing (1-2 kg/plant) = 20-60 kg
- Year 4-5: Full production (2-4 kg/plant) = 40-120 kg

**Revenue Year 4** (30 plants mature):
- Fresh sales: 60 kg × €20/kg = €1,200
- Dried sales: 60 kg fresh → 15 kg dried × €60/kg = €900
- **Total**: €1,200-1,800 (from 50m² plot!)

**IF scale to 200 plants Year 4-5**: €8,000-15,000/year potential

**Scalability**: **4/5** - GOOD

**TOTAL SCORE**: **21/25** - RECOMMENDED

## CATEGORY 5: GRAINS/PSEUDOCEREALS (Specialty Markets)

### 12. **QUINOA** (Chenopodium quinoa) ⭐⭐⭐

**Origin**: Andes Mountains (Peru, Bolivia)
**Why Test**: Superfood trend, gluten-free, complete protein, growing market

**Climate Compatibility**: **3/5** - MODERATE-CHALLENGING
- Cold-tolerant (can handle light frost)
- Grows best in cool climates (day temp 15-25°C = spring/fall in Miranda)
- Challenge: Hot summers can reduce yields (harvest before peak heat)
- Requires 90-120 days frost-free (you have enough)
- Drought-tolerant (once established)

**Market Potential**: **4/5** - GOOD
- Grain: €4-8/kg wholesale, €8-15/kg retail
- Growing demand (gluten-free, vegan protein, health food)
- Currently imported from South America or France/Spain
- "Portuguese quinoa" = local marketing advantage
- Competition: Some production starting in Spain, Portugal (but still small)

**Cultural Difficulty**: **3/5** - MODERATE
- Annual crop (plant spring, harvest fall)
- Similar to spinach/beets (same family)
- Challenge: Saponins in seed coat (must wash before eating - processing step)
- Bird protection needed (birds LOVE quinoa - netting required)
- Harvest: Cut seed heads when mature, dry, thresh

**Scalability**: **4/5** - GOOD
- 100m² = 30-80 kg grain
- 500m² = 150-400 kg = €600-3,200/year
- Mechanizable at larger scale (combine harvester)

**Uniqueness**: **3/5** - MODERATE
- Some Portuguese/Spanish quinoa appearing
- Market not saturated, but no longer novel

**TOTAL SCORE**: **17/25** - MARGINAL

**Recommendation**: **LOWER PRIORITY** - Only test if very interested in grains OR have bird control solution
- Better alternatives for labor/revenue: Edamame, goji, hardy kiwi
