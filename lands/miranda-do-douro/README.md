# Miranda do Douro Region

**District**: Bragança, Trás-os-Montes, Northeastern Portugal
**Border**: ~5km from Spain
**Climate**: Continental Mediterranean (Köppen Csb)

## Why This Region

- **Climate advantage**: 800-1,200 chill hours — ideal for premium tree fruits (apples, cherries, chestnuts)
- **Cold winters**: Natural pest suppression, less pesticide needed
- **Hot dry summers**: Intense herb/grape flavors, less fungal disease
- **Low land prices**: Rural depopulation = affordable farmland (€2,000-10,000/ha)
- **Cross-border market**: Spain 5km away, EU single market access
- **Underserved niches**: Brazilian immigrant community in Portugal (200,000+), zero competition for specialty herbs
- **Douro tradition**: Wine region heritage, grape-growing expertise

## Challenges

- Summer drought (Jun-Sep <50mm) — irrigation essential
- Shallow rocky soils on slopes
- Rural depopulation — limited local services
- Building rights complexity (solo rústico, REN/RAN)
- Distance from major cities (Bragança ~40km, Porto ~200km)
