## SKILL 5: LAND EVALUATOR

### Current Strengths
- Excellent 100-point scorecard (8 categories, well-weighted)
- Clear buy/maybe/reject recommendation framework
- Strong "critical unknowns" checklist
- Good financial overview integration
- Comprehensive development roadmap format
- Realistic red flag system

### Gaps Identified

#### GAP 5.1: Storage Infrastructure Assessment Missing
**Discovered in**: INSIGHTS_FROM_1_8_ACRE_FAMILY_GARDEN.md

**Missing**:
- Root cellar viability as scored category
- Natural cold storage potential (earth temperature at depth, unheated buildings)
- Curing space assessment (covered, ventilated, protected from rain)
- Storage capacity estimation (m³ usable, kg capacity by crop)
- Winter in-ground storage potential (clamps, mulch-covered beds)

**Recommended Addition — New Sub-Score in Infrastructure (10 points)**:
```markdown
### Storage Infrastructure Potential (within Infrastructure 10 points)

**Assessment**:
- Root cellar possible: Earth-contact possible? Hillside? Basement?
- Curing space: Covered area with ventilation available?
- Clamp storage: Winter temperatures allow in-ground storage?
- Unheated building storage: Existing structures usable for squash/onions?

**Scoring impact**: Property with root cellar potential scores +1-2 points on Infrastructure vs property without.
```

#### GAP 5.2: Value-Added Processing Potential Not Scored
**Discovered in**: INFUSED_PRODUCTS_EQUIPMENT_ANALYSIS, ROSEMARY_BEEKEEPING_SOAP_ANALYSIS

**Missing**:
- Processing kitchen viability in existing buildings
- Water/power adequacy for processing
- Regulatory pathway clarity for processing (food vs cosmetics)
- Proximity to inspection services
- Multi-enterprise processing potential (shared facility)

**Recommended Addition — New Sub-Score in Enterprise Compatibility**:
```markdown
### Value-Added Processing Potential

Score 0-5:
- 5: Existing legal processing facility or clear path, all utilities
- 4: Building exists, minor renovation for processing, legal pathway clear
- 3: Building exists but needs significant work, legal pathway likely
- 2: No existing building, legal pathway uncertain, utilities limited
- 1: Processing not viable — building, legal, or utilities barriers
- 0: Processing prohibited

**Impact**: Processing can 3-10× raw product value. This sub-score significantly affects income potential.
```

#### GAP 5.3: Market Niche Proximity Assessment
**Discovered in**: HERB_PRODUCTION_ANALYSIS (Brazilian market), multiple analyses

**Missing**:
- Demographic niche proximity scoring (immigrant communities, tourist areas)
- Geographic market advantages (border proximity, urban distance)
- Underserved market identification (what's NOT available locally)
- Direct-to-consumer channel viability (farmers markets, farm gate traffic)
- Restaurant/tourism market proximity

#### GAP 5.4: Climate Advantage Scoring
**Discovered in**: NON_NATIVE_HIGH_POTENTIAL_CROPS_ANALYSIS (cold-hardy advantage)

**Missing**:
- Chill hours as positive asset (not just constraint)
- Cold winter advantages (pest suppression, dormancy quality, crop quality)
- Continental climate advantages (flavor development, storage quality)
- Heat unit accumulation scoring (growing degree days)
- Climate uniqueness as market advantage (grows what others can't)

**Recommended Addition**:
```markdown
### Climate Advantages Scoring (within Climate Suitability 10 points)

**Beyond "suitable/not suitable" — what does this climate do BETTER than others?**

- High chill hours (800+): Premium apples, cherries, chestnuts — crops warm regions can't grow
- Cold winters: Natural pest suppression, less pesticide needed
- Hot dry summers: Intense herb flavors, less fungal disease
- Continental swing: Excellent for storage crops (dry harvest, cold storage)
- Frost: Kills pests, improves soil structure through freeze-thaw

**Score boost**: +0.5-1.0 on Climate Suitability when climate provides unique competitive advantage.
```

#### GAP 5.6: Water Storage & Harvesting Potential Detail

#### GAP 6.1: Equipment ROI Calculation Templates
**Discovered in**: INFUSED_PRODUCTS_EQUIPMENT_ANALYSIS.md

**Missing**:
- Standardized equipment ROI formula
- Payback period calculation by equipment type
- Multi-enterprise equipment utilization (one machine, many products)
- Equipment progression costing (DIY → Prosumer → Professional)
- Revenue per €1,000 equipment investment benchmarks

**Recommended Addition**:
```markdown
### Equipment ROI Calculator

**Formula**:
```
Annual Revenue from Equipment - Annual Operating Cost
ROI = --------------------------------------------------- × 100
              Equipment Cost
```

**Benchmarks**:
- Dehydrator (€400): Revenue €14,550/yr → ROI 3,090-4,040%
- Ultrasonic Infuser (€250): Revenue €15,000-20,000/yr → ROI 5,088-10,263%
- Vacuum Sealer (€200): Value preservation + packaging → ROI 960-1,800%
- Soap Making Setup (€300): Revenue €16,000-28,000/yr → ROI 5,233-9,233%

**Equipment Sharing Multiplier**:
One dehydrator serves: herbs + fruit + mushrooms + jerky + flowers
Revenue multiplier: 2-4× vs single-use equipment

**Decision Thresholds**:
- ROI >1,000%: Buy immediately (pays back in weeks)
- ROI 500-1,000%: High priority (pays back in 1-3 months)
- ROI 200-500%: Good investment (pays back in 3-6 months)
- ROI 100-200%: Consider if multi-enterprise use
- ROI <100%: Only if essential and no alternative
```

**Discovered in**: ROSEMARY_BEEKEEPING_SOAP_ANALYSIS, INFUSED_PRODUCTS_EQUIPMENT_ANALYSIS

**Recommended Addition**:
```markdown
### Value-Added Margin Calculator

**Example: Rosemary — Raw vs Value-Added**

| Product | Raw Material | Processing | Packaging | Labor (hrs) | Total Cost | Sale Price | Margin | Margin % |
|---------|-------------|------------|-----------|-------------|------------|------------|--------|----------|
| Fresh rosemary (1kg) | €0 (grown) | €0 | €0.50 | 0.2 (€2) | €2.50 | €8 | €5.50 | 69% |
```

#### GAP 6.3: Storage Crop Economics
**Discovered in**: INSIGHTS_FROM_1_8_ACRE_FAMILY_GARDEN.md

**Missing**:
- Storage value appreciation (sell in Feb at 1.5-2× harvest price)
- Root cellar as appreciating asset (saves €X/year in purchased food)
- Storage loss rates (5-15%) vs fresh waste rates (20-40%)
- Labor comparison: storage crops vs fresh vegetables (hours/kg)
- Land efficiency: calories/m² for storage vs fresh crops

**Recommended Addition**:
```markdown
### Storage Crop Economics

**Root Cellar ROI**:
- Construction: €1,500-3,000 (DIY-mid range, 10m²)
- Annual value stored: 300-500kg food
- Retail replacement value: €1,200-2,500/year
- Payback: 1.2-2.5 years
- Lifespan: 30+ years (essentially permanent)
- 30-year ROI: 4,000-8,000%

**Storage Price Appreciation**:
- Potato harvest price (Sep): €0.60/kg
- Potato winter price (Feb): €1.00-1.20/kg
- Storage premium: 67-100% for waiting 4-5 months
- Cost of storage: Near zero (passive root cellar)

**Labor Efficiency Comparison** (per 100kg food produced):
- Storage potatoes: 8-12 hours (plant, occasional weed, harvest 1 day, cure, store)
- Fresh salad greens: 40-60 hours (sequential planting, weekly harvest, wash, pack)
- Storage crops = 4-5× more food per labor hour
```

## SKILL 7: PHASED IMPLEMENTATION PLANNER

### Current Strengths
- Excellent 4-phase framework (0-4)
- Strong sequencing principles (dependencies respected)
- Good seasonal timing guide
- Solid "common mistakes" section
- Example phased plan provided

### Gaps Identified

#### GAP 7.1: Storage Infrastructure in Phase 1-2
**Discovered in**: INSIGHTS_FROM_1_8_ACRE_FAMILY_GARDEN.md

**Missing**:
- Root cellar in Phase 1 or early Phase 2 (before first big harvest)
- Curing space setup before storage crop harvest
- Storage crop integration in Year 1 vegetable plan
- Clamp storage as temporary solution while root cellar built

**Recommended Addition**:
```markdown
### Storage Infrastructure Timeline

**Phase 1 (Months 0-6)**:
- Build temporary curing area (covered, ventilated space): Month 1-2
- Plant storage crops (potatoes, carrots, onions, squash): Month 2-3
- Begin root cellar construction: Month 3-5 (before first harvest)
- Harvest and cure storage crops: Month 5-6
- Fill root cellar: Month 6

**If root cellar can't be built in Phase 1**:
- Clamp storage (in-ground): €0, temporary
- Unheated room in existing building (squash, onions only)
- Process/preserve instead of fresh store (dehydrate, can)

**Rationale**: Storage crops harvest in Sept-Oct. Root cellar MUST be ready. Building it in spring = ready for fall.
```

#### GAP 7.2: Processing Equipment Progression
**Discovered in**: INFUSED_PRODUCTS_EQUIPMENT_ANALYSIS.md

**Missing**:
- Equipment acquisition phasing (DIY → Test → Professional)
- Which equipment in which phase
- Revenue thresholds for equipment upgrades
- Multi-enterprise equipment scheduling by phase

**Recommended Addition**:
```markdown
### Equipment Acquisition Phasing

**Phase 1 — DIY/Test** (Budget: €200-500):
- Window screen drying (herbs, fruit) — €0-20
- Basic knife/cutting board processing — existing
- Mason jar infusion (herbs in oil/vinegar) — €20-50
- Simple packaging (paper bags, twine, labels) — €50-100
- **Purpose**: Test products, validate market, learn techniques

**Phase 2 — Prosumer** (Budget: €500-1,500, trigger: €3,000+ sales):
- Electric dehydrator (Excalibur 9-tray) — €350-450
- Vacuum sealer — €150-250
- Digital scale, better packaging — €100-200
- Ultrasonic infuser (Altafuse or similar) — €200-300
- **Purpose**: Scale proven products, consistent quality

**Phase 3 — Professional** (Budget: €1,500-5,000, trigger: €10,000+ sales):
- Commercial dehydrator — €1,000-2,000
- Professional packaging (heat sealer, label printer) — €500-1,000
- Dedicated processing kitchen (if legal pathway clear) — €3,000-10,000
- **Purpose**: Full commercial production, wholesale capacity
```
