## The Water Advantage — What Changes Everything

Spec 5 is the only property with **both** well and stream. This changes the development timeline:

| Water Task | Spec 2 (public water) | Spec 5 (well + stream) |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Drinking water | Connected | **Well — test + pump** |
| Irrigation | Public (metered, €) | **Stream — gravity/ram pump, FREE** |
| Livestock water | Public (metered) | **Stream — direct access, FREE** |
| Irrigation cost/year | €200-400 | **€0** (stream, no pump needed if gravity) |
| Water security | Depends on public supply | **Independent — well + stream redundancy** |
| Summer drought | Public may restrict | **Stream may slow but well = backup** |

**Stream-fed irrigation alone saves €200-400/year vs public water. Over 20 years: €4,000-8,000.**

## Phase 1: First 30 Days — Water + Food

### Day 1-7: Water System Assessment

**Well testing** (PRIORITY — before purchase ideally):
- Flow rate test (L/hour). Target: >1,000L/hour for domestic + garden.
- Water quality test (potability, minerals, contaminants). €50-150 lab test.
- Pump: submersible electric or hand pump. €300-1,500 depending on depth.
- **If well underperforms**: stream is backup for all non-drinking uses.

**Stream assessment**:
- Flow rate (summer minimum vs winter maximum)
- Elevation drop across property (gravity-fed irrigation potential)
- Ram pump viability (if elevation exists — pumps water uphill with zero electricity)
- Water rights: riparian — check with APA

### Day 1-14: Immediate Food Production

**Fast crops with stream irrigation**:
- No waiting for well pump — stream water immediately available
- 50m² fast crops: radish (21 days), salad mix (30 days), cilantro (30-45 days)
- Direct-sow near stream for easy watering
- **Investment**: €50-100. **Revenue**: €200-500 within 30-60 days.

**Market garden layout** (300m²):
- Lay out beds parallel to stream for easy gravity-fed drip lines
- Flat or gentle slope = ideal for gravity irrigation
- 15 beds × 1m × 15m + paths
- **Investment**: €200-400 (drip tape, seeds, amendments)

### Day 1-14: Chickens

- 8-10 point-of-lay pullets: €80-120
- Mobile coop (DIY): €100-250
- Stream = drinking water for chickens (no carrying water)
- **Revenue**: €90-180/month in eggs from Month 2

**Phase 1 Total**: €500-1,000. Revenue Month 1-2: €300-700.

## Phase 3: 90 Days - Year 2 — Building Systems

### Water Infrastructure

**Stream-fed irrigation system**:
- Gravity-fed drip lines from highest stream point: €300-500
- OR ram pump to elevated tank → gravity distribution: €500-1,000
- No electricity needed if gravity works
- **This is the spec_5 advantage**: zero-cost irrigation forever

**Well pump + storage**:
- Submersible pump: €300-800
- Storage tank (1,000-2,000L): €200-500
- Pressure system for house: €300-600
- **Total water infrastructure**: €1,100-2,900

### Beekeeping

2-4 hives. Stream = year-round bee water source (critical in summer).
**Investment**: €550-850. **Year 2+**: €500-2,000.

### Processing Setup — No Building Challenge

Spec_5 has no building (unlike spec_2's kitchen+bath). Options:

**Option A — Temporary/Modular** (Year 1-2):
- Tool shed (10-15m²): €1,000-2,500. Can house dehydrator, basic processing.
- Covered outdoor processing area (near stream, shade): €300-800.

- Registered kitchen = Tier 2+ products (infused oils, preserves)

**Recommendation**: Start Option A (dried herbs only). Transition to Option B when dwelling built.

**Equipment** (works in shed or outdoor area):
- Dehydrator: €400

- Mason jars + packaging: €150
- **Total**: €750
